# Clinic Owner Language Playbook

## Credentials and Professional Titles
- PT: Licensed physical therapist
- DPT: Doctor of Physical Therapy degree
- MSPT: Master of Science in Physical Therapy
- MPT: Master of Physical Therapy
- PTA: Physical therapist assistant
- OT: Occupational therapist
- COTA: Certified occupational therapy assistant
- ATC: Athletic trainer certified
- CSCS: Certified strength and conditioning specialist
- OCS: Orthopedic clinical specialist
- SCS: Sports clinical specialist
- WCS: Women's health clinical specialist
- GCS: Geriatric clinical specialist
- NCS: Neurologic clinical specialist
- FAAOMPT: Fellow of the American Academy of Orthopaedic Manual Physical Therapists
- COMT: Certified orthopedic manual therapist
- Cert MDT: McKenzie method certified
- Residency trained: Completed advanced specialty training
- Fellowship trained: Highest post professional specialty training

## Clinical Terms and Treatment Lingo
- Eval: Initial evaluation visit
- Re eval: Follow up reassessment
- POC: Plan of care for treatment
- Progress note: Required status update for payors
- SOAP note: Standard documentation format
- Manual therapy: Hands on treatment techniques
- Ther ex: Therapeutic exercise
- Ther act: Functional movement based activities
- NMR: Neuromuscular reeducation
- Modalities: Passive treatment tools
- E stim: Electrical stimulation
- Dry needling: Needle based trigger point treatment
- IASTM: Instrument assisted soft tissue work
- Joint mobs: Joint mobilizations
- Return to sport: Rehab for athletic clearance
- Outcomes measures: Standardized patient score tools
- FOTO: Functional outcome tracking system
- Oswestry: Low back disability index
- NDI: Neck disability index
- LEFS: Lower extremity functional scale

## Billing, Coding, and Reimbursement
- CPT codes: Procedure billing codes
- ICD 10: Diagnosis billing codes
- Units: Billable time increments
- Timed codes: Time based billing codes
- Untimed codes: Flat rate billing codes
- 8 minute rule: Medicare time calculation rule
- Authorization: Insurance approval for visits
- Medical necessity: Justification for care
- Modifier 59: Distinct procedural service modifier
- KX modifier: Exception to therapy cap modifier
- RVU: Relative value unit for services
- Allowed amount: Insurance approved payment
- Write off: Difference between charge and allowed
- Credentialing: Getting approved with payors
- EOB: Explanation of benefits
- Denial: Rejected insurance claim
- AR: Accounts receivable balance
- Days in AR: Average time to collect
- Clean claim: Error free submission

## Insurance and Payor Language
- Commercial insurance: Private insurance plans
- Medicare: Federal insurance for seniors
- Medicaid: State based low income coverage
- Workers comp: Work injury insurance
- PI case: Personal injury claim
- Fee for service: Paid per visit model
- Value based care: Outcomes driven payment model
- Payor mix: Breakdown of insurance types
- Copay: Fixed patient payment
- Deductible: Amount patient pays first
- Coinsurance: Percentage patient responsibility

## Cash Pay and Hybrid Models
- Cash based: No insurance billing
- Out of network: Does not contract with payor
- Superbill: Receipt for patient reimbursement
- Hybrid clinic: Mix of cash and insurance
- Concierge PT: Premium one on one model
- Membership model: Recurring monthly payment
- Wellness program: Post rehab maintenance care
- Visit bundle: Package of prepaid visits
- Paid in full discount: Incentive for upfront payment

## Clinic Operations and Metrics
- Visits per week: Total patient visits weekly
- Visits per new patient: Average visits per case
- Completion rate: Percent finishing plan of care
- Arrival rate: Percent who show up
- Cancellation rate: Percent who cancel
- No show rate: Missed appointments
- Units per visit: Billing intensity metric
- Revenue per visit: Average collected per visit
- Schedule density: How full the schedule is
- Double booking: Two patients at same time
- Mill clinic: High volume overlap model
- One on one care: Single patient per hour
- Provider capacity: Max visits clinician can handle
- Break even: Revenue covers expenses
- Overhead: Fixed operating costs
- EBITDA: Earnings before interest taxes depreciation amortization

## Staffing and Growth
- Clinical director: Lead clinician manager
- Per diem: As needed hourly staff
- 1099 contractor: Independent contractor
- W2 employee: Traditional payroll employee
- Productivity standard: Expected visit quota
- De novo: Brand new startup clinic
- Acquisition: Buying existing clinic
- Roll up: Consolidating multiple clinics
- MSO: Management services organization
- Platform clinic: Anchor location for expansion

## Marketing and Patient Acquisition
- Physician referral: Doctor sending patients
- Injury screen: Free assessment event
- SEO: Organic search ranking strategy
- PPC: Paid advertising traffic
- Cost per lead: Marketing spend per inquiry
- Conversion rate: Lead to patient percentage
- Reactivation: Bringing back past patients

## Common Industry Slang
- The mill: High volume insurance clinic
- Chasing units: Maximizing billable codes
- Stacking patients: Overlapping appointments
- Underwater with Medicare: Low margin visits
- Feeding the schedule: Filling open slots
- Swiss cheese schedule: Scattered openings
- Fighting denials: Appealing rejected claims
- Cash unicorn: High profit cash clinic
